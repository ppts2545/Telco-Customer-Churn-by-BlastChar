Business Insights – Customer Churn Analysis

Dataset: Telco Customer Churn

---

## Insight 1: Paperless Billing Behavior

Customers who do not use paperless billing tend to have no internet service.
These customers usually subscribe to fewer services and therefore have lower
monthly charges.

As a result, this customer segment shows a lower churn rate compared to
customers who use paperless billing.

Business Interpretation:
Low-service customers have lower costs and fewer active services,
which may reduce the likelihood of churn.

---

## Insight 2: Internet Service Dependency

Customers without internet service do not subscribe to streaming services
such as StreamingTV or StreamingMovies.

This confirms that the dataset follows the expected business logic:
streaming services require an active internet service.

Business Interpretation:
Streaming services are dependent features tied to internet subscriptions.

---

## Insight 3: Internet Service and Churn Risk

Customers who subscribe to internet services show a higher churn rate
compared to customers who only use phone services.

Internet subscriptions typically involve higher monthly charges due to
additional services such as streaming or higher bandwidth plans.

Business Interpretation:
Higher service costs may increase churn risk, especially when customers
perceive the price as too high relative to the value received.

---

## Insight 4: Monthly Charges and Customer Tenure

Monthly charges and customer tenure appear to be key factors associated
with churn behavior.

Customers with higher monthly charges tend to churn more frequently,
while customers with longer tenure generally show lower churn rates.

Business Interpretation:
Customers who stay longer with the company become more stable and loyal,
while high monthly costs may increase dissatisfaction and churn risk.

---

## Insight 5: Contract Length and Churn

Contract type is strongly related to churn behavior.

Customers with shorter contracts (such as month-to-month) tend to have
significantly higher churn rates compared to customers with longer
contracts (one-year or two-year agreements).

Business Interpretation:
Short-term contracts provide flexibility for customers to leave easily,
while longer contracts help improve customer retention.

---

## Conclusion

Customer churn is influenced by a combination of service usage, pricing,
and contract structure.

Customers with more services (especially internet) tend to have higher
monthly costs and a greater risk of churn, while long-term contracts and
longer customer tenure are associated with stronger retention.




---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Business Recommendations

1. Encourage Long-Term Contracts
   Customers with month-to-month contracts show significantly higher churn
   rates compared to those with one-year or two-year contracts.

Recommendation:
Offer incentives such as discounts or bundled service benefits to
encourage customers to switch to longer-term contracts. This can help
improve customer retention and reduce churn risk.

---

2. Reduce Price Sensitivity for High Monthly Charges
   Customers with higher monthly charges tend to churn more frequently,
   especially among those using internet services.

Recommendation:
Introduce bundled service packages or loyalty discounts for customers
with higher monthly charges. Providing additional value may help reduce
price sensitivity and improve customer satisfaction.

---

3. Focus on Retaining Internet Service Customers
   Customers who subscribe to internet services have a higher churn rate
   compared to customers who only use phone services.

Recommendation:
Improve service quality, customer support, and value-added features
for internet users. Enhancing the perceived value of internet services
can help reduce churn among this important customer segment.

---

4. Strengthen Early Customer Retention
   Customers with shorter tenure tend to have a higher churn rate,
   suggesting that new customers are more likely to leave.

Recommendation:
Implement early-stage retention strategies such as onboarding support,
promotional offers, or loyalty incentives during the first months of
service to increase long-term customer commitment.

---

5. Maintain Stability of Low-Service Customers
   Customers who do not use paperless billing often have fewer services
   and lower monthly charges, and they tend to churn less frequently.

Recommendation:
Maintain competitive pricing and simple service plans for this
low-service segment to preserve their loyalty and prevent unnecessary
churn.
